Tutorial (Level 1-10)

  • (Optional) Pick any starting class you want. If you're not sure, I recommend starting with Vagabond or Samurai, as they have good starting stats for most builds.
  • (Optional) For a completionist's run, I recommend starting with the Stonesword Key x2 keepsake. They are necessary items for full completion and two of them costs at least 4,000 runes, meaning you won't need to buy an extra two for an expensive price in the early game. You could also pick the Lands Between Rune for 3,000 runes, but if you have to buy Stonesword Keys at any point, then I think picking those will give you greater value in the long run.
    • Note: Don't pick the Golden Seed if you're following this guide, as it will become obsolete almost immediately after the first few sections. No starting class equipment or keepsake is missable, so go with whichever one you like!
  • Start your playthrough, and pick up Tarnished's Wizened Finger from the chapel.
  • The Ring gesture is aqcuired if you pre-ordered the game.
  • Similarly, the Ring of Miquella gesture is aqcuired if you pre-ordered the DLC.
  • Continue toward the boss, and either beat or die to the Grafted Scion.
    • If you beat the boss, continue ahead and inspect the nascent butterfly at the edge of the cliff.
    • If you didn't beat the boss, don't worry, you're supposed to come back here later.
  • After respawning in the cave, take the plunge down to the Cave of Knowledge.
  • Rest at the Cave of Knowledge site of grace and allocate your flask charges.
  • There are no items in this cave, so once you are done, kill Soldier of Godrick for 400 runes.
  • Continue ahead and pick up the Strength! gesture before you drop down.
  • Drop down and activate the Stranded Graveyard site of grace.
  • There is an Imp Seal statue here you can unlock with your Stonesword Keys, but ignore this for later when you're much stronger.
  • Pick up Finger Severer and Tarnished's Furled Finger in one pickup, near the exit lift.
  • Take the lift up and enter the Lands Between!

Early Liurnia and Caelid (Level 1-10)

  • Note: Reminder that you should try to avoid fighting any enemies while you are in Liurnia and Caelid early. You will pretty much get one-shot by most enemies, so just run through each step here without fighting anything.

  • Take in the view and once you're ready, head southwest to the church below. Head inside the church and pick up the Sacred Tear.
  • Talk to Thops, give him 10 runes to unlock his inventory, and buy any sorceries you need from him:
    • Glintstone Pebble: 1,000 runes
    • Glintstone Arc: 1,500 runes
    • Starlight: 1,500 runes

Vigor: The stat you should focus on early. Getting 40 Vigor early makes the entire early-mid game a complete joke. Combined with the fact you can start the game with a +5 Somber weapon and plenty of upgraded Flasks, you will be tanking and killing most enemies with little investment. It is not recommended to level Vigor past 60.

Mind: Although the scaling dropoff on Mind happens quite late, most builds will be fine around 20-40 Mind.

Endurance: Level based on how much stamina and equip load your build needs. You typically want to end around 40-50 Endurance.

Strength: Strength scaling largely depends on your weapon and its individual scaling when infused with an Ash of War affinity. For an accurate scaling, use a Build Planner, add your weapon and play with the Strength stat until you notice diminishing returns. Keep in mind that two-handing your weapon multiplies your Strength stat by 1.5x. For example, with 20 Strength, two-handing would raise it to 30, allowing you to wield weapons that require more Strength without actually meeting the stat requirements.

Dexterity: Same as with Strength. Dex scaling depends on your weapon and its individual scaling. Use a Build Planner, add your weapon of choice and play with the Dexterity stat until you notice diminishing returns.

Intelligence: For the individual scaling on each staff, I highly recommend checking out Fextra's page about Intelligence.

Faith: For the individual scaling on each seal, I highly recommend checking out Fextra's page about Faith.

Arcane: If your weapon scales with Arcane, then leveling this stat will increase the amount of Blood Loss, Poison, Sleep, and Madness buildup your weapon inflicts. A list of all weapons that scale with Arcane can be found on Fextra's page. Arcane is also the only stat that increases your Discovery (1 per point).

General recommendation:

  1. Reach level requirements for your desired weapon
  2. Focus exclusively on vigor. Leveling any of the damage stats yield little value, since most weapons have poor scaling early on
  3. Level Endurance for your equip load with armor and weapon
  4. Start focusing on your dedicated stat, once your weapon has better scaling

What's included in the playthrough?

The playthrough DOES NOT include:

  • Any farmable items, plants, and crafting materials collected from the ground (materials picked off the wall or ground such as smithing stones or gloveworts ARE included)
  • Any respawning scarabs such as red and blue teardrop scarabs that fill your flasks
  • Any stranded souls/ghosts or sword monuments that you can interact with for optional lore
  • Any martyr effigies that you can interact with to activate co-op summon-signs for an area
  • Any guide and gatekeeper statues, birdseye telescopes, or stakes of marika
  • Any farmable weapons, shields or armor that drop as loot from enemies are not included in the playthrough, but can be found in the Armaments and Armor tabs respectively

The playthrough DOES include:

  • Every single item pickup in the game (glowing pickups that you can interact with to receive an item)
  • All achievements, bosses, NPCs, invasions, and their corresponding questlines in natural order
  • All merchants and their items in order of when you'd come across them
  • All sites of grace, gestures, ashes, paintings and talismans
  • All scarabs that drop loot
  • All illusory walls and secrets
  • All scadutree fragments, revered spirit ashes and miquella's crosses from the DLC

How do I use the cheat sheet?

This project is a comprehensive walkthrough guide for Elden Ring, covering every single item pickup, NPC questlines, bosses, achievements and more. If this is your first playthrough, I highly recommend that you complete the game on your own first and then use this guide for your second run.

In the main Playthrough tab, you have a step by step checklist guide through the entire game. You can filter this checklist to only show steps for bosses, questlines, achievements or key-items, ideal if you don't want to follow the guide, but still want to get the important stuff. Additionally, you can use the search bar to only show items matching your search. For example, typing "Stonesword Key" in the search field and filtering by Merchants would show you which merchants sell stonesword keys.

The Achievements, Armaments, Armor, and Misc tabs serve as separate checklists for tracking achievements, weapons, armor, gestures, ashes, and more.

In the Resources tab, you'll find tables with useful information about the game's stat caps, upgrade materials, rune items and more.

If you don't like the notebook theme, you can switch to dark mode by clicking the moon icon in the navigation bar. Click the sun icon to switch back.

Do I have to follow every step in order?

Not necessarily, this project was primarily intended for my cousin and me. Since I am a completionist, I like to make sure that I don't miss a single detail the game has to offer. This guide represents how I prefer to complete the game, reducing as much backtracking as possible and approaching the game's areas in a natural, yet efficient order. Elden Ring is a vast open world game, so if you'd rather play at your own pace, but still don't wanna miss any important questlines or achievements, give some of the filters a try.

Will I get all achievements if I follow the guide?

Yes! All achievements, including every step to get them are covered in the Playthrough tab. If you're following the guide and playing on PlayStation, you'll naturally get the Platinum Trophy just by following each step. You can filter the playthrough to only show achievement-related steps and which order you'll get them in. Check out the Achievements tab for a checkbox list of all the achievements.

Are there any missable things in Elden Ring?

Yes, many things in Elden Ring are missable. While most of them are quest-related, some achievements can be permanently missed until your next playthrough. Every missable entry is clearly labeled (Missable!), so that they can be easily searched and filtered by typing "missable" into the search bar. Missable entries that don't give any item or reward are labeled (Optional). You won't miss anything if you're following the guide.

How long does it take to 100% complete Elden Ring?

A 100% completion playthrough of Elden Ring following this guide, including getting all three endings in a single run, typically takes between 60-90 hours, accounting for variables like differences in playstyles and player skill. The guide is optimized to avoid unnecessary backtracking where possible, while approaching the game in a natural order.

What level should I be for each area?

The guide provides recommended level ranges for each area in Elden Ring. These ranges do not represent the difficulty of an area, but rather the level you're likely to reach naturally when following the guide. These are suggestions based on typical progression from various of my own playthroughs, and could help you avoid being over- or under-leveled if you want a fair experience.

What do I do if I accidentally attacked an NPC?

You should never attack or kill an NPC in Elden Ring unless it's specifically stated to do so in the guide. If you've attacked any NPCs and they've become hostile, then you'll need to travel to Church of Vows in Liurnia and absolve your sins in front of the statue there by using a Celestial Dew. If you've already killed an NPC, then their questline is over until you start a new playthrough. Make sure to pick up their bell bearing if they were selling any items.

How do I navigate in Elden Ring using compass directions?

Elden Ring's compass uses primary, intercardinal and secondary-intercardinal directions. I strongly recommend keeping the compass HUD enabled as the guide uses it to provide accurate directions. Below are the tiers used to help provide directions:

  1. Primary Cardinal Directions:
    • North (N)
    • East (E)
    • South (S)
    • West (W)
  2. Intercardinal Directions:
    • Northeast (NE)
    • Southeast (SE)
    • Southwest (SW)
    • Northwest (NW)
  3. Secondary Intercardinal Directions:
    • North-Northeast (NNE)
    • East-Northeast (ENE)
    • East-Southeast (ESE)
    • South-Southeast (SSE)
    • South-Southwest (SSW)
    • West-Southwest (WSW)
    • West-Northwest (WNW)
    • North-Northwest (NNW)

For an in-game example, northeast refers to the midpoint between north and east. Similarly, north-northeast would then refer to the midpoint between north and northeast.
